Tips and tricks

Gender Rules of Thumb

Time & Weather: Days of the week, months, and seasons are generally masculine.
Natural Features & Locations: Rivers, towns, and trees are often feminine.
Words ending in -en, -aeth, -fa, -es, or -ell are often feminine (e.g., cacen, swyddfa).
Words ending in -yn, -wr, -deb, or -der are often masculine (e.g., pysgodyn, cyflymder).


The Mutation Trick

Singular feminine nouns mutate after the definite article y (the), while masculine nouns do not.
Feminine: cath (cat) = y gath (the cat - 'c' mutates to 'g')
Masculine: ci (dog) = y ci (the dog - no mutation)
Note: Feminine words starting with "ll" or "rh" are exceptions and do not mutate after "y" (e.g., y llyfrgell).


The Adjective Trick

Use Adjectives: Learn the noun with an adjective. If the adjective changes (mutates), the noun is feminine.
Feminine: merch dda (good girl, 'd' of 'da' changed to 'dd')
Masculine: bachgen da (good boy, 'd' stays 'd')


The Number Trick

Use Numbers 2, 3, 4:
Dwy (2), Tair (3), Pedair (4) are used with feminine nouns.
Dau (2), Tri (3), Pedwar (4) are used with masculine nouns.
Feminine: Dwy gath (2 cats)
Masculine: Dau gi (2 dogs)
